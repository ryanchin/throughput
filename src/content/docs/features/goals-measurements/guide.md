# How to Use the Goal & Metric Planner

This guide walks you through creating a complete strategic planning package — SMART goals, OKRs, and KPIs — using the Goal & Metric Planner.

## Step 1: Enter Your Business Context

Start by providing the context that will shape your goals:

- **PM Role / Domain** (required) — The area you're planning for
- **Customer Problems** (optional) — Key problems your customers face
- **Company Strategy** (optional) — Strategic context or priorities from leadership
- **Team Charter** (optional) — Your team's mission and focus areas
- **Baselines** (optional) — Current metrics and benchmarks to build targets from

The more context you provide, the more relevant and specific your goals will be.

### Examples of Great Input

**PM Role / Domain:**
- "Enterprise Platform — responsible for API infrastructure, developer experience, and third-party integrations"
- "Growth — focused on trial-to-paid conversion, activation, and self-serve expansion revenue"

**Customer Problems:**
- "Enterprise customers are churning after year one because they can't demonstrate ROI to their CFO. They need usage analytics dashboards and executive-ready reports they can share internally."
- "Mid-market teams adopt 2-3 features during onboarding but never discover the remaining 80% of the platform. Feature discovery and progressive disclosure are broken."

**Company Strategy:**
- "CEO has set a company-wide priority to move upmarket from SMB to enterprise. We need to support SSO, audit logging, and role-based access by Q3. Net new ARR target is $2M from enterprise logos."

**Baselines:**
- "Current NPS: 34. Trial-to-paid conversion: 12%. Average time-to-value: 21 days. Feature adoption beyond core: 23%. Monthly active users: 4,200."

## Step 2: Review Your Complete Planning Package

Product Studio generates your entire strategic planning package in one pass:

- **SMART Goals** (3-5) — Each with title, description, priority, category, timeframe, and success criteria
- **OKRs** — Objectives and Key Results aligned to each goal, with baseline and target values
- **KPIs** — Primary and secondary KPIs linked to each Key Result, with measurement methods and check frequency

Everything appears in your results automatically — you don't need to run separate tools for OKRs or KPIs.

## Step 3: Refine Any Part of the Plan

You have three ways to refine at any level (goals, OKRs, or KPIs):

- **Manual editing** — Click into any field to edit directly
- **AI revision** — Use the revision panel to request changes. Describe what you want different and the AI will regenerate. Revising a Key Result automatically updates its linked KPIs to stay aligned.
- **AI questions** — Ask clarifying questions (detected automatically when your input contains a "?") without changing anything

Every revision is tracked in the revision history. You can revert to any previous version.

## Step 4: Export and Share

Export your complete plan as a PDF or Word document for stakeholder review. The export includes goals, OKRs, KPIs, and alignment information in a single document.

## Tips

- Start with 2-3 high-quality optional fields rather than filling everything with thin content
- Use the revision panel to iterate — don't try to get it perfect on the first generation
- Provide baselines when possible — this helps the AI set realistic targets
- Use the feedback button to help improve future generations
