# Feasibility Analyzer

Evaluates whether a feature is technically feasible — with effort estimates, timeline scenarios, risk assessment, skills analysis, and a clear Go / No-Go / Conditional recommendation.

## Questions It Answers

- "Can we actually build this? How long will it take?"
- "What are the major technical risks, and how do we mitigate them?"
- "How many people do we need, and what skills are required?"
- "What's the optimistic vs. pessimistic timeline?"

## Example Use Cases

- A PM wants to bring a feature proposal to the executive team but needs credible effort estimates and a risk assessment first.
- A VP of Engineering is evaluating three potential projects and needs side-by-side feasibility comparisons to inform portfolio investment decisions.
- A CTO's office debating build-vs-buy wants an objective technical breakdown before making the call.

## What Makes It Special

- Three-point estimation (optimistic, realistic, pessimistic) for every component
- Auto-detects the likely tech stack
- Maps required skills to proficiency levels and person-weeks
- The Go/No-Go recommendation comes with a confidence level so you know how much weight to put on it
