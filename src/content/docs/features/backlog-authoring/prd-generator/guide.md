# How to Use the PRD Generator

This guide walks you through creating a comprehensive Product Requirements Document with knowledge base context, citations, and professional exports.

## Step 1: Provide Your Business Concept

Enter your business concept or requirements (required). This can be a brief feature description, a problem statement, or a detailed set of requirements.

Optionally enhance with:

- **Import Source** — Pull context from Ideation, Scope Definition, or Goal Setting sessions
- **Target Persona** — Who the feature is for
- **Industry Context** — Relevant industry details
- **Primary Metric** — The key metric this feature should move
- **Knowledge Bases** — Attach organizational docs for citation-backed generation
- **File Attachments** — Upload supporting documents (maximum 20MB per file)

### Examples of Great Input

**Business Concept:**
- "Build a usage analytics dashboard for enterprise customers that shows product adoption metrics, team activity, and ROI calculations. The dashboard should help customer success managers identify at-risk accounts before renewal. It must support SSO-authenticated access, allow customers to export reports as PDF, and include benchmark comparisons against anonymized industry averages. Primary audience is the VP-level buyer who approved the purchase and needs to justify renewal to their CFO."

**Target Persona:**
- "VP of Operations at a mid-market SaaS company (200-500 employees). Non-technical but data-literate. Cares about team productivity metrics, cost savings, and board-ready reporting. Currently uses a patchwork of spreadsheets and manual Jira exports."

**Primary Metric:**
- "Net Revenue Retention — this dashboard should directly reduce churn by making ROI visible, and indirectly drive expansion by surfacing unused features customers are paying for."

## Step 2: Review the 10 Sections

Product Studio generates up to 10 best-practice PRD sections. Each includes:

- Structured content following PM best practices
- **Inline citations** from your knowledge bases (clickable to view source excerpts)
- Acceptance criteria in Given/When/Then format where applicable

## Step 3: Edit and Refine

- **Manual editing** — Click into any section to edit directly (supports markdown). Only one section can be in edit mode at a time.
- **Section-specific AI refinement** — Revise a single section without affecting others
- **Global AI refinement** — Revise the entire PRD at once
- Questions are answered without modifying the PRD; revision requests update it

## Step 4: Export

- **PDF export** — Color-coded sections with citation references
- **Word export** — Same formatting in .docx format

Both formats include full citation details for traceability.

## Tips

- Attach knowledge bases whenever possible — citations dramatically improve PRD credibility
- Import from Scope Definition for the tightest alignment between scope and requirements
- Use section-specific refinement to iterate on one area without disrupting the rest
- The Given/When/Then acceptance criteria are ready for direct use in sprint planning
- Export to Word when stakeholders need to add comments or track changes
