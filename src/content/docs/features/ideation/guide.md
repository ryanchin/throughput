# How to Use the Ideation Engine

This guide walks you through generating scored, categorized solution ideas from a problem statement.

## Step 1: Define the Problem

Write a clear problem statement (minimum 100 characters). The more specific and well-framed, the better the ideas.

You can also **import context** from:

- **Goals / OKR & KPI** sessions — Ground ideation in strategic objectives
- **Market Research** — Use market insights as the problem foundation

### Examples of Great Problem Statements

**Strong — specific, measurable, and contextual:**
- "Enterprise customers (500+ seats) are churning at 18% annually despite high product satisfaction scores. Exit interviews reveal the primary driver is inability to demonstrate ROI to finance stakeholders. We need solutions that make value visible and quantifiable without requiring customers to build their own reporting."
- "Our mobile app has a 4.2 star rating but 34% of 1-star reviews mention the same issue: the onboarding flow requires 14 steps and takes an average of 23 minutes. Users who don't complete onboarding within 48 hours have a 78% drop-off rate. We need to radically simplify first-run experience."

**Weak — too vague for quality ideas:**
- "We need to improve retention" (no specifics about who, why, or what's been tried)
- "Mobile app needs work" (no data, no user insight, no constraint framing)

**Constraints (optional but powerful):**
- "Must work within our existing React Native app. Cannot require backend changes — we're in a code freeze until March. Budget for this initiative is one designer and two engineers for 4 weeks."

## Step 2: Add Optional Context

- **Knowledge Bases** — Attach organizational docs for domain-aware ideas
- **Constraints** — Technical, budget, or timeline limits that shape realistic solutions
- **Goals** — Strategic goals the ideas should align with
- **Research Insights** — Market or customer research to inform the ideas

## Step 3: Explore the Results

You'll receive 15-16 ideas organized into 3-5 thematic clusters, automatically prioritized by score. Each idea includes:

- **Scores** across five dimensions: impact, feasibility, effort, strategic fit, and risk
- **Category label** — Quick Win, Strategic Bet, Incremental, or Moonshot (auto-assigned based on scores)
- **Use cases** — When and how the idea would be applied
- **Edge cases** — Potential complications or exceptions
- **Implementation notes** — High-level technical direction

## Step 4: Edit and Refine

- Expand any idea to see and edit its full details
- Use **AI refinement** to request changes — revisions update the ideas, questions get answers without changes
- Focus on the ideas that scored highest across your priority dimensions

## Step 5: Continue Downstream

Top ideas can flow into:

- **Scope Definition** — Draw boundaries around what makes the cut for v1
- **Feasibility Analysis** — Evaluate technical buildability of top-scoring ideas
- **Epic / Feature / Story Creator** — Turn ideas into backlog-ready artifacts

## Tips

- A well-written 100+ character problem statement is the single biggest lever for quality output
- Don't dismiss Moonshots — they often contain kernel ideas that become practical when descoped
- Use the cluster view to spot patterns — if multiple ideas cluster around one theme, that's a strong signal
- Import from Market Research when available — it dramatically improves idea relevance
