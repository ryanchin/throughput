# How to Use the CX Research Planner

This guide walks you through designing a complete customer research study — from method selection to ready-to-use research instruments.

## Step 1: Define Your Research Objective

Describe what you're trying to learn.

### Examples of Great Research Objectives

**Strong — specific and actionable:**
- "We redesigned the enterprise onboarding flow from 14 steps to 5, but we haven't shipped it yet. We need to validate that the simplified flow doesn't confuse IT administrators who are setting up SSO and provisioning users. Key risk: we removed the configuration wizard and replaced it with smart defaults — will admins trust it?"
- "Our self-serve trial converts at 12% but the industry benchmark is 22%. We suspect the problem is between 'first login' and 'first meaningful action' — users create an account but don't complete the setup wizard. We need to identify the specific drop-off points and understand why users abandon."

**Weak — too broad:**
- "Understand the user experience" (which users? which part of the experience?)
- "Get customer feedback" (about what? for what decision?)

## Step 2: Set Constraints

Provide your practical constraints:

- **Budget** — How much you can spend on research
- **Timeline** — When you need results by
- **B2B or B2C** — This tailors the methodology recommendations

## Step 3: Select Research Methods

Product Studio recommends methods based on your objective and constraints. Common recommendations include usability studies, customer interviews, surveys, card sorting, and A/B testing.

Select the methods you want to pursue.

## Step 4: Review Generated Instruments

For each selected method, the system generates the actual research tools:

- **Interview scripts** with screener questions and probing follow-ups
- **Surveys** with response options and skip logic
- **Recruiting plans** with outreach email templates and participant criteria

## Step 5: Execute and Iterate

Use the generated instruments directly, or refine them with AI before fielding. Results can inform downstream flows like Journey Mapping and Ideation.

## Tips

- Start with your objective, not your preferred method — let the constraints guide methodology
- B2B mode produces significantly different instruments than B2C — make sure you select the right one
- Use the recruiting email templates as-is — they're optimized for response rates
- Run research before committing to a direction — it's far cheaper to learn early
