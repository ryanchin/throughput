# How to Use the Journey Mapper

This guide walks you through creating AI-powered customer journey maps with stages, touchpoints, emotion curves, and pain points.

## Step 1: Describe the Journey

Provide a journey description or import context from:

- **Scope Definition** — Use project scope as journey context
- **Feasibility Analysis** — Map the journey around a specific feature
- **Ideation** — Explore journeys related to solution ideas

Optionally attach knowledge bases and supporting files.

### Examples of Great Journey Descriptions

**Standard Mode:**
- "Map the end-to-end journey of an enterprise IT administrator purchasing and deploying our platform. Starting from the moment they first hear about us (conference, peer referral, or G2 review) through vendor evaluation, security review, procurement, initial deployment, team rollout, and first quarterly review with their VP."

**Multi-Persona Mode:**
- "Map the same enterprise deployment journey for three personas: (1) IT Admin who handles the technical setup and SSO configuration, (2) the End User PM who will use the platform daily, and (3) the VP of Product who approved the purchase and needs to see ROI. Show where their experiences diverge."

**Competitive Mode:**
- "Compare our customer onboarding journey vs. Productboard's onboarding journey for a mid-market product team (20-50 users). Focus on time-to-first-value, setup complexity, and the 'aha moment' that drives adoption."

## Step 2: Explore the Journey Map

The output includes:

- **Sequential stages** with detailed descriptions
- **Emotion curve** — Visualized emotional state across the journey
- **Pain points** — Each scored by severity (0-10) and marked as hypothesis or data-backed
- **Touchpoints** — With channel identification and strength/weakness flags
- **Evidence sources** — Excerpts and attribution for data-backed insights

## Step 3: Edit and Refine

- Edit pain point severity and descriptions directly
- Use **AI revision** for global or stage-targeted refinements
- Questions are answered without changing the map; revision requests update it

## Step 4: Export

Export as PDF for stakeholder presentations.

## Tips

- Use Multi-Persona mode for enterprise products where buyers and users are different people
- Mark insights as "hypothesis" vs "data-backed" honestly — it builds credibility with stakeholders
- Focus on high-severity pain points first — they're your biggest improvement opportunities
- Import from Scope Definition to ensure the journey map covers the right boundaries
