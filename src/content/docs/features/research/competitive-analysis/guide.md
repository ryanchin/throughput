# How to Use Competitive Analysis

This guide walks you through researching competitors and generating stakeholder-ready competitive intelligence.

## Step 1: Describe the Problem Space

Describe the feature area or problem space you want to research competitively. The more specific, the more useful the output.

### Examples of Great Input

- "We're building a collaborative whiteboarding feature for remote enterprise teams. Analyze how Miro, FigJam, and Lucidspark approach real-time collaboration, template libraries, and enterprise admin controls. Focus on gaps in their offerings for regulated industries."
- "Our analytics dashboard competes with Mixpanel, Amplitude, and Heap. We need to understand how each approaches enterprise-grade permissions, data governance, and custom dashboards to find our differentiation angle."

## Step 2: Add Context

- Optionally import context from feasibility analyses, business cases, or PRDs to make the research more targeted
- Select knowledge bases for organizational context

## Step 3: Review Results

The analysis covers competitor approaches, feature gaps, positioning, pricing strategies, and market positioning — all organized for quick scanning.

## Step 4: Generate Presentations

Use the PowerPoint export to create stakeholder-ready competitive intelligence decks.

## Tips

- Import from a PRD or Business Case to get competitor analysis that's laser-focused on your specific feature area
- Combine with Market Research Synthesizer for a complete competitive landscape view
- Run competitive analysis before major product decisions to ensure you're not building into a crowded space
