# How to Use the Market Research Synthesizer

This guide walks you through generating data-backed market insights for any problem area.

## Step 1: Define Your Research Area

- **Problem Area** (required) — Describe the market problem or opportunity you're investigating
- **Industry** (required) — Select from the industry dropdown to focus the research
- **Import Source** (optional) — Pull context from Ideation, OKR, or Scope Definition sessions

### Examples of Great Input

**Problem Area:**
- "Enterprise customers are increasingly demanding AI-powered automation in their project management tools. We're evaluating whether to build native AI features or integrate with third-party AI providers. Need to understand where the market is heading and what customers actually expect vs. what's hype."
- "Our compliance management module is losing deals to specialized GRC platforms. We need to understand how the compliance software market is evolving, what regulatory changes are driving adoption, and whether we should double down or partner."

**Industry Selection Tips:**
- Choose the industry your *customers* operate in, not your own. If you sell project management software to healthcare companies, select "Healthcare" — the research will focus on PM trends within healthcare.

## Step 2: Select Focus Areas

Choose at least one focus area for the research:

- **User Expectations** — How customer needs are shifting
- **Adoption Trends** — What's gaining traction in the market
- **Market Risks** — Threats and uncertainties
- **Regulation** — Regulatory changes that could affect your space
- **Technology Shifts** — Emerging tech that could disrupt or enable

By default, **User Expectations**, **Adoption Trends**, and **Market Risks** are pre-selected. Adjust based on what's most relevant to your research question.

## Step 3: Review Findings

Results are organized into tabbed sections:

- **Market Trends** — What's happening now
- **Expectation Shifts** — How customer needs are changing
- **Market Risks** — What could go wrong
- **Strategic Implications** — What this means for your product

Every finding includes a **confidence level** (High, Medium, Low) and **source attribution** with relevance scores.

## Step 4: Filter and Refine

- **Filter by confidence** — Focus on High-confidence findings for executive presentations, or include Medium/Low for broader exploration
- **AI refinement** — Ask follow-up questions or request deeper analysis on specific findings
- **Export as PDF** for sharing with leadership

## Tips

- Use confidence filtering before presenting to executives — lead with High-confidence findings
- Combine with Competitive Analysis for a complete market picture
- Import from Ideation to ground research in the specific problems you're trying to solve
- Run research early in the product lifecycle — insights compound across downstream flows
