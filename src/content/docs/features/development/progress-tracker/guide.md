# How to Use the Progress Tracker

This guide walks you through tracking sprint progress with AI-powered blocker detection and risk scoring.

## Step 1: Connect Your Project Management Tool

Connect to Jira or Azure DevOps to pull live sprint data. Configure which project and board to track.

## Step 2: Select a Sprint

Choose an active sprint to monitor. The Progress Tracker pulls in all items, their statuses, assignees, and movement history.

## Step 3: Review Sprint Health

The dashboard shows:

- **Sprint burndown** — Actual vs. ideal progress
- **Item status breakdown** — To Do, In Progress, Done, Blocked
- **Risk flags** — AI-detected items at risk of not completing
- **Blocker detection** — Items that haven't moved, are missing assignees, or show concerning patterns
- **Velocity trends** — How this sprint compares to historical averages

## Step 4: Generate Status Reports

Generate stakeholder-ready status reports with one click. Reports include sprint progress, key accomplishments, blockers, and risks — formatted for executive or team-level audiences.

## Step 5: Take Action

Use the insights to:

- Reassign blocked items
- Escalate risks to leadership
- Adjust sprint scope if the commitment is at risk
- Document decisions for retrospectives

## Tips

- Check the Progress Tracker daily during active sprints — early blocker detection is its highest-value feature
- Use the generated status reports for standup prep and stakeholder updates
- Compare velocity trends across sprints to identify systemic capacity issues
- Items flagged as "at risk" deserve immediate attention — the AI flags them for a reason
